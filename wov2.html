In February 2015, I co-founded a Facebook group with one other person called Women of Vienna. It started as a longing for a community that offered support, connectivity, and the opportunity to make friendships. We were soon asked to organize in-person events, the first of which 40 women attended. Our events became so popular that we took on a third team member to help us with administrative tasks and hosting.


For me, it was clear from the very beginning that we would have to concretely define our values and goals to keep the community from developing into something we did not want. This meant sticking to those values and goals even when it was difficult, while at the same time maintaining an open mind and a sense of flexibility. My main role was defining the guidelines under which the group functioned and developing a concrete identity for the community. Soon, recurring themes started to appear and I developed a model for topic subgroups, or smaller themed communities.


Five years later, we reached over 20,000 members in our main group and 11 topical subgroups led by a team of 25+ volunteers. Our non-profit organization status was officially recognized by the governing body in Vienna in 2019. We became beta testers for new Facebook Groups features and one of our team was a part of a development group run by Facebook. We built a visual language to accompany the brand of Women of Vienna and began to focus more on our public facing representation of the community.


The most valuable lessons I learned from developing and leading this group include; how to work with an incredibly diverse team, how to hack an existing platform (Facebook) to fit individual needs, and how to keep the big picture in mind when focusing on details. Today I am immensely proud of Women of Vienna, the lives it has changed and the good it has done. It has shown me that being intentional, consistent, and persistent is the best way to build a quality product.


Women of Vienna was initiated in February 2015 by Betsy Akins and Michelle Hrvat. It was recognized as an official non-profit organization in 2019.
