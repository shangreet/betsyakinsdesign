This project began by using Kendon's concept of F-formations to observe human behavior in public spaces. My team observed Kamppi during three time slices; 11:00, 17:00, and 23:00. We used our observations in a Google Design Sprint to define a focus area and develop a product. Our chosen topic was personal space and power dynamics.


Our concept, Solu, was a complete interactive system that includes auditory, visual, and tactile elements. It is designed to be implemented in public spaces where visitors have to wait, such as public transit stations which include shopping options. It includes three linked features, the first being an interactive touchscreen map of the area which can indicate which areas are more densely populated and guide visitors to a quiet area. The second feature is interactive personal circle lights which can be activated and manipulated to convey a desire to be left alone. When occupied, these circles relay information to the map about how occupied that area is. The final feature is a responsive sound system that adjusts ambient background noise levels based on information from the circles and map to set sound levels to a calm, relaxing state.


After the Google Design Sprint we decided to do a vertical prototype and focus on the interactive light circle element. This element was the most interesting to our evaluators because of the embodied method of interaction. We did a rough prototype of the interaction in Figma before handing it over to the evaluation team.


My Role: Communication expert - during these three weeks I organized and conducted evaluation interviews, planned concise and informative reports, and presented our work to the class. I personally created the storyboards which acted as the narrative behind our prototype.
